Notes and Legend
--------------
"Across The Plain is quiet, lyrical and reflective, depicting a family on a
wagon journey across the warm wide-open plains and prairies of America. The
musical feel is spacious and cinematic, reflecting the lyrical, sustained
sounds of a string orchestra. I was in the process of writing this movement
when I heard the news on September 11th 2001." - Gary Ryan

many of the natural (open) harmonics are played just with the right hand
many of the grace notes are played as slides

measure 80 - the second chord is to be held for a long time

left hand fingering (below the tab)
1 - index finger
2 - middle finger
3 - ring finger
4 - little finger

===    - note held
/      - slide up
g      - grace note - acciaccatura - an extra note which is played just before,
         or at the same time as the main note, and is immediately released
h      - hammer-on
p      - pull-off
arp.   - arpeggiate - ripple upwards from the lowest string
<12>   - open (octave) harmonic on the 12th fret
<19>   - open harmonic on the 19th fret
2<14>  - artificial harm. - left hand 2nd fret, right hand points to 14th fret
5<17>  - artificial harm. - left hand 5th fret, right hand points to 17th fret
CII    - barre on the 2nd fret with the 1st finger
F.     - Fermata - pause, hold notes
mf     - mezzo forte - moderately loud
mp     - mezzo piano - moderately soft
p      - (below the tab) piano - soft
pp     - pianissimo - very soft
< < <  - crescendo (growing louder)
> > >  - decrescendo (growing softer)
