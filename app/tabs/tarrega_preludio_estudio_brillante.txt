"Preludio in A major" by Francisca Tarrega (1852-1909)

Submitted By: Gezgin Paksoy - Jan 2008

The Prelude in A major, like Chopin's first prelude, is full of expectation
and short as a sigh. (This is a direct quote from the guitarist Fabio Zannon
who recorded the piece.)

It is a really short prelude, but fun to play. It is also proof that Tarrega
can concentrate his musical ideas into a few bars. It has to be played with an 
accelerating speed. Begin slowly. Gently accelerate until the third measure
and after the third or fourth measure mantain a constant tempo until the end.
The MIDI is a bit slow. It originally takes 19 seconds to play this piece. :)

PS Tarrega wrote many preludes as an introduction to his other longer pieces.
I think this prelude is played before his "Estudio Brillante".
