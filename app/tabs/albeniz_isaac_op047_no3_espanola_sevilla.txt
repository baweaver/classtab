 Isaac Albéniz - Sevilla (Sevillanas)

 Tabbed by The_S (April 2003)

 The_S: This piece I consider to be the greatest guitar solo I have ever heard
        I first heard it played by John Williams, who pronounces his notes
        with outstanding accuracy and clarity, and who never fails to follow
        the tempo. Then I heard Andres Segovia playing it, who plays it
        with elegance, straying from the mathematical precision of tempo
        to show emotion.
        Up until now (April 2nd, 2003), I have not been skilled enough to
        play Sevilla, because it involves many quick chord changes and
        much coordination in the left hand.
        I am pleased to have finished memorizing it, and can't wait to play
        it as well as John Williams does.
        You will notice a few notes in brackets e.g. --(0)---
        These are notes not mentioned in the sheet music, but which are played
        by John Williams instead of the other notes on the tab and sound just
        as good, so they are optional.
        Also, watch the beat measures at the top of the tabs, I have had
        to space them out sometimes to make the notes easier to read
        Otherwise, good luck and enjoy.

 Tuning: DGDGBE
