Although a few tabs of this timeless and moving piece by Erik Satie exist,
this is another arrangement that is performed by Christopher Parkening.
Personally, I feel it is a much more expressive arrangement whose "difficult"
parts are easier to play.

One should liberally exercise "vibrato" in this piece to emphasize and bring
out the theme notes or chords. This piece should be played slowly and with
great feeling.

Paul R. Estrada - November 2002
estrada@cosmic.arc.nasa.gov

Gymnopedie 1 - Eric Satie

Several guitar tablatures of this hypnotic, minimalist melody by
Eric Satie can be found on the net, but I didn't like any (no offense).
Problem? The atmosphere and harmonies created at the piano by long
notes are hard to mimic with a guitar unless you transpose to an
adequate tonality.

This tab is based on the original piano score (in D), but the main
influence is from a recording by master Angel Romero. It uses normal
tuning and E tonality, which allows frequent use of open strings for
long notes. Romero's playing is so clear that you almost 'see' his
fretting, so it was an easy task. And a nice thing is that the
fingering is so simple a kid could play it.

Comments and improvements welcome
