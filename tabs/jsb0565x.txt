Arreglo for solo guitar, Alejandro Santander - alesantan@hotmail.com

Symbols:

p    -> The note next to the symbol is to be played with the thumb.
p/   -> Straight down with the thumb.
<.>  -> Alternate middle and index fingers, RESTING them, on each note, on 
the single
string above the string just played. (Always accentuated).
</>  -> Alternate middle and index fingers, WITHOUT accentuation. (Simple 
technique).
>>>  -> The sound is to continue until...
X*Y  -> Note Y is not to be 'picked' by the right hand.
C    -> Bar.
C'   -> 1/2 Bar.

Toccata (and Fugue) in Dm
BWV 565
by Johann Sebastian Bach (the greatest composer who ever lived)

Submitted by:  Ben Pratt
jaminben@aol.com

I have seen a few transcriptions of this song in the Guitar/Tab directory,
and I would like to submit my version. Unlike the other transcriptions 
I have seen, this one is in Dm, but it is virtually impossible to play
the Fugue in Dm, with the counterpoint, on the guitar, so this is simply the
Toccata. I was originally turned on to this piece by the obscure heavy metal
band 'Cirith Ungol' who has a version of this song on their CD 'King of the
Dead'. My version is very similar to theirs, except that mine is for solo
guitar whereas theirs was for two guitars and a bass. I think they also used
an ebow, which gave the song a neat tone. The piece is written in Common
Time(4/4) and begins Adagio. One thing to keep in mind is that adagio in
the Baroque Period was faster than that in following eras, so it should
be played at about 80bpm. In measures six - eight, the rhythmic pattern is
16th triplet, 16th triplet, 16th triplet, 16 duple, 16th triplet, 16th
triplet, 16th triplet, 16th duple. It gives the section a very forced
sound and adds to the feeling of solemnness that horror movie composers have
liked for so long. Measures 9-13 are the most difficult of the whole song.
I have tried tapping it, but it presents the problem of running up and
needing to be picked at the beginning of each run with another finger.
If anyone can find an easier way to play it, please let me know. Their are
also 4 or 5 measures near the end that I did not include because it sounds
very weak without both staves and is impossible to play without 2 guitars.
One other thing to keep in mind is that the rhythm sounds very broken
because it is riddled with fermatas and other devices. Instead of worrying
about the time, play it how you think it should feel.

Oh yeah, drop the low E to a D.

I hope you enjoy it and I would appreciate any comments.

Toccata from Toccata And Fuge in D minor by J.S. Bach

When I was looking on the net for some classical pieces arranged for
guitar I seen this one and decided to see how other people play it.
I seen a great number of versions so I decided to tab out the way I
play it. I use an electric guitar to play this. It sounds very close
to the original, in my opinion. I'm not going to give right hand
fingering because I don't know how to play with my fingers, I play
this with a pick. At times it can be extremely difficult, but it can
be done. I'll tab out the Toccata, but I will not tab out the Fuge.
It is way too long. Finally, I just wanted to say that this is my 
first time actually tabing out a song in this fashion so try to bear
with me.

If you have any questions you can e-mail me at knight69@bellatlantic.net

*You'll have to listen to the piece to get the rhythm right.  I'm
not going to bother writing it out.  Just a hint though, pretty 
much every note is either, sixteenth, thirtysecond, or sixtyfourth.*

h = hammer on
p = pull off
s = slide
NH = harmonic
T = trill
F = fermata
> = accent

*** You can do the trills two ways. On some versions played by the organ,
they only do what would be the equivilent to one hammer on and one pulloff
on guitar. The other way they do it is to make them very drawn out. ***

Drop D tunning: 6=D
