El Ultimo Tremolo (or...Una Limosnita por el Amor de Dios).
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~
by Agustin Barrios Mangore (1885-1944)

About the origin of the piece:

Agustin Barrios Mangore lived the last few years of his life in the city
of San Salvador, El Salvador, surrounded by friends and pupils.
One evening whilst teaching, an old lady knocked on the door to beg for 
an alm (limosna).  Her exact pleading words were: "UNA LIMOSNITA POR EL 
AMOR DE DIOS".  After the class, the knocks on the door inspired the
tremolo masterpiece.  The knocks are apparant in the first two bars of
the piece and they resound throughout the entire work beneath the
beautiful yet desparate melody.  This was to be Agustin's last composition
for he died a month later.

BTW, I did NOT make this up... honest.  

Tabulated by David Atkinson (email: kaum0@central.sussex.ac.uk) on 11/10/94.
V2.0 (11/94)
This is my fourth transcription and has to be my most ambitious yet.
It is a tremolo piece.  Tremolo means "Trembling, rapid repetition of note".
It is a difficult technique to master at first so perseverence is the key.
Each beat is divided into four notes, the thumb playing the base note
followed by a m i (ring, middle & index), repeating the top note.
To save space and time, I have used the following notation (t=tremolo).

p  a  m  i
      |   |   |   |   |   |           "|  |  |  |"
E-|---t12----this means play--|    E-----12-12-12--------------|
B-|----------the open low E---|    B---------------------------|
G-|----------string and then--| =  G---------------------------|
D-|----------the high E at the|    D---------------------------|
A-|----------12th fret three -|    A---------------------------|
E-|---0------times in rapid---|    E---0-----------------------|
             succession as
             described above.
)
( = arpeggiate
)  

==== = sustain

I have included left hand fingering beneath the tab.  1 is for the
index finger, 2 is middle and so on.  It should be self explanatory.
I have split the fingering into two levels, the tremolo level and 
the bass note level.  A 't' precedes the tremolo fingering.

So here it is, the masterpiece itself.  Please email me if you find
any mistakes.  The time is 3/4.  It is in the key of E minor although
it changes halfway into the key of E major.  ENJOY!!!

El Ultimo Tremolo (or...Una Limosnita por el Amor de Dios).
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~
by Agustin Barrios Mangore (1885-1944)

This is tabbed according to the great performance by Denis Azabagic on the DVD,
Guitar Foundation of America: International Guitar Competition Winners, Vol 1.
Some of the fingering is tough and may be thought mistaken. Azabagic obviously
wanted to keep the tremolo on the high E string as much as possible, which is
nice. Pay very close attention when comparing the fingering with other versions
on classtab.org, because some of the differences are subtle - but absolutely
crucial for smooth transitions. Special thanks to David Atkinson for the fine
template. Enjoy! - Jackson Eskew (January 2007)

A few tips -

Bars 3-4 (& 19-20) - lead with the left elbow for a smooth transition to
                     bars 4 (& 20).
Bar 45 - sequential fingering eases this passage.
Bars 47-50 - keep left hand straight, parallel with the frets; resist the
             temptation to pronate the wrist.
